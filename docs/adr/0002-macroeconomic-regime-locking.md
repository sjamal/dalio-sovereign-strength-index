# ADR 0002: Macroeconomic Archetypal Regime Bounding

## Context and Problem Statement
When running simulations over standard 10-to-20 year timeline views, users observe that structural lifecycle categorizations remain entirely fixed (e.g., US bounded to Stage 5, China bounded to Stage 4). We must document if this constitutes a code execution bug or structural theoretical intent.

## Decision Outcome
This behavior is correct and matches the design principles of the *Changing World Order* framework. 

Ray Dalio's "Big Cycles" reflect historical multi-variable systems tracking a 200-to-250 year duration. Short-term timeseries data windows capture long-term macro structures that fluctuate very slowly over generational limits. The model correctly identifies that the contemporary United States remains stuck in Stage 5 due to unliquidated fiscal leverage bounds, while China remains locked inside Stage 4 due to ongoing infrastructure spending velocity.
